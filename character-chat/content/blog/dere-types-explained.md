# Tsundere vs Yandere vs Kuudere: Every Dere Type Explained

If you've spent any time in anime communities, you've probably heard terms like "tsundere" or "yandere" thrown around. But what do they actually mean?

This guide explains **every dere type** with examples, so you'll never be confused again.

## What Does "Dere" Mean?

"Dere" (デレ) comes from "deredere" (デレデレ), a Japanese onomatopoeia meaning to be lovestruck or lovey-dovey. 

All "dere" types describe how a character **expresses affection**, usually with a contrasting trait attached.

---

## The Main Dere Types

### 1. Tsundere (ツンデレ)

**Meaning:** Tsun (aloof/cold) + Dere (lovestruck)

**Behavior:** Initially cold, hostile, or dismissive, but gradually warms up and shows their loving side.

**Classic line:** "It's not like I like you or anything, baka!"

**Examples:**
- Taiga Aisaka (Toradora!)
- Asuka Langley (Evangelion)
- Tohsaka Rin (Fate/Stay Night)

### 2. Yandere (ヤンデレ)

**Meaning:** Yanderu (mentally sick) + Dere (lovestruck)

**Classic line:** "I'll kill anyone who tries to take you from me."

### 3. Kuudere (クーデレ)

**Meaning:** Kuuru (cool) + Dere (lovestruck)

**Behavior:** Calm, emotionless, and composed on the outside. Rarely shows emotion but is secretly caring.

**Classic line:** *silent stare* "...I don't hate you."

**Examples:**

### 4. Dandere (ダンデレ)

**Meaning:** Danmari (silent) + Dere (lovestruck)

**Behavior:** Shy, quiet, and introverted. Opens up only around people they trust.

**Classic line:** "I-I'm not good with words... but I like being with you."

**Examples:**
- Hinata Hyuga (Naruto)
- Nagisa Furukawa (Clannad)
- Mio Akiyama (K-On!)

## Other Dere Types

### 5. Himedere (姫デレ)
**Meaning:** Princess + Dere

Acts like royalty and expects to be treated as such. Demands attention and service.

**Example:** Erina Nakiri (Food Wars)

### 6. Kamidere (神デレ)
**Meaning:** God + Dere

Has a god complex. Believes they're superior to everyone else.

**Example:** Light Yagami (Death Note)

### 7. Bakadere (バカデレ)
**Meaning:** Idiot + Dere

Lovable but clumsy and dim-witted. Makes silly mistakes.

**Example:** Yoshiko Hanabatake (Aho Girl)

### 8. Deredere (デレデレ)
**Meaning:** Pure Dere

Simply loving, affectionate, and openly romantic. No hidden personality.

**Example:** Orihime Inoue (Bleach)

### 9. Sadodere (サドデレ)
**Meaning:** Sadist + Dere

Shows love through teasing, manipulation, or mild cruelty.

**Example:** Kurumi Tokisaki (Date A Live)

### 10. Mayadere (まやデレ)
**Meaning:** Deceiver + Dere

Starts as an enemy but falls in love and switches sides.

**Example:** Esdeath (Akame ga Kill)
